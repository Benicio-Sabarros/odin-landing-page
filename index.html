Local cuisine

Here are some local bavarian dishes you'll want to try out during your stay.

Apple Strudel

A german classic, sweet and equally soft and crunchy, you can find this delicious dessert on every restaurant in Bavaria. Recipe.

Apfelstrudel or apple strudel is a traditional dessert typical of Austrian and southern German cuisine, whose origins could date back to ancient recipes from Byzantine, Armenian or Turkish cuisine. -Wikipedia

Bavarian Pretzels

You have probably already tried a Pretzel at least once, but these salty dough wonders are even better in Bavaria. Recipe.

A pretzel is a type of baked pastry made from dough that is commonly shaped into a knot. The traditional pretzel shape is a distinctive symmetrical form, with the ends of a long strip of dough intertwined and then twisted back onto itself in a particular way (a pretzel loop or pretzel bow). -Wikipedia

Bavarian Bratwurst and Bacon Burger

Burgers come from Hamburg, Bratwursts from Nuremberg, Bacon comes from ...China? And they all taste great together. Recipe.

Bratwurst is a type of German sausage made from pork or, less commonly, beef or veal. The name is derived from the Old High German Brätwurst, from brät, finely chopped meat, and wurst, sausage. Beef and veal are usually incorporated amongst a blend often including pork. -Wikipedia
